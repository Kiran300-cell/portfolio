What Makes a Recruiter Stay on a Portfolio for More Than 30 Seconds

People stay when a site makes them feel oriented, confident, and curious.

Attention is earned quickly. The first screen should communicate role, strengths, and next steps without making visitors guess. If your site feels confusing or unfinished, many people will leave before seeing your work.

Recruiters also respond to proof. Concrete projects, thoughtful writing, and clear contact information create confidence. The goal is not just to impress but to reduce uncertainty.

Good portfolios create momentum. They guide a visitor from headline to evidence to action in a way that feels natural rather than forced.
